Lab 01 - How Marijuana Affects Self-Reported Health Status

Introduction

In this lab, we will attempt to provide an answer to the question, “Do regular users of marijuana have a different level of self-reported health than people who report never using marijuana?”

Data

To address this question, we will use data from the 2014 National Survey on Drug Use and Health, which is conducted annually by the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA), an agency in the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS). More information about this data can be found at this link.

Data Management

load("36361-0001-Data.rda") #load data
full_data <- da36361.0001 #rename data
remove(da36361.0001) #remove old data
library(tidyverse)
## Loading tidyverse: ggplot2
## Loading tidyverse: tibble
## Loading tidyverse: tidyr
## Loading tidyverse: readr
## Loading tidyverse: purrr
## Loading tidyverse: dplyr
## Conflicts with tidy packages ----------------------------------------------
## filter(): dplyr, stats
## lag():    dplyr, stats
regular_use_criteria <- 15
regular_users <- filter(full_data, MJDAY30A >= regular_use_criteria)
never_users <- filter(full_data, MJEVER == "(2) No")

We defined regular users as those who used marijuana at least 15 days out of the past 30.

Graph Creation

ggplot(regular_users)+
  geom_bar(aes(x=0,fill=HEALTH, y=..count../sum(..count..)))+ 
  geom_bar(data=never_users,aes(x=1,fill=HEALTH, y=..count../sum(..count..)))+
  scale_fill_brewer(palette="RdBu",direction=-1)+
  scale_x_continuous(breaks=c(0,1), labels=c("Regular Users", "Never Users"))+
  labs(title="Self-Reported Health Status", x="User Category", y="Relative Frequency")

To create this graph, I used the ggplot and geom_bar functions to create two side by side barplots that compare the relative frequencies of the self-reported health statuses of regular users of marijuana and those who have never used marijuana. I used the scale_fill_brewer and scale_x_continuous functions to make the bar colors a red-blue gradient (red being the poorest health) and give the bars labels.

Results

Regular users of marijuana do have a different level of self-reported health than people who report never using marijuana. The data shows that more people who have never used marijuana report having “excellent” and “very good” health than those who are regular users of marijuana. Those who have never used marijuana also report their health as “fair” and “poor” less frequently than regular users.
